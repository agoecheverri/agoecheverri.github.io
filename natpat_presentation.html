Does Patriotism Depolarize? two Pre-Registered Tests and a New Diagnostic for Experimental Nulls

Ago Echeverri, Leonie Huddy, Rongbo Jin

Stony Brook University

2026-03-31

The Problem

Motivation

  • Define AP briefly: the gap between positive feelings toward inparty and negative toward outparty
  • Consequences: undermines democratic norms, legislative gridlock, fear of outparty government
  • This is the problem we are trying to solve

The intuitive fix: remind Americans of what they share

  • Social Identity Theory → Common Ingroup Identity Model (Gaertner et al. 1993)
  • Logic: if partisan identity is causing the animosity, making a superordinate identity salient should reduce it
  • “We are all Americans first”

The track record is inconsistent

Study Effect on outparty affect
Levendusky (2018) β = 0.056
Voelkel et al. (2024) β ≈ 0.075
Jin et al. (2025) null
Versteegen et al. (2025) null

Brandt et al. (2020) null
  • Levendusky found modest positive effect — replications have failed
  • Voelkel added methodological improvements, found effect — but treatment is complex
  • Something is wrong with how we conceptualize the treatment

Design limitations

  • Levendusky (2018) uses a long vignette (631 words) in bullet-point format, then asks people to list reasons why “America is Great”.

  • Voelkel et al. (2024) use an even longer treatment composed of diverse screens, but mix up national primes with attitude polarization correction.

  • Furthermore, both experimental designs do not run manipulation checks — it is unknown whether nulls happened by inattentiveness or not

Our Design

Kept from prior work

  • Vignette format
  • Forced exposure time
  • Writing task after treatment
  • Feeling thermometer outcomes

What we changed

  • Shorter texts
  • Excluded inattentive respondents pre-treatment
  • Writing task used as Factual Manipulation Check and reinforcement task
  • Focus on a single facet of National Identity; Images chosen to match frame only
  • The key innovation is frame purity — every element of the treatment pulls in the same direction
  • The writing task serves double duty: engagement + manipulation check

Exclusion: Mock Vignette Check (mock vignette)

  • Fail mock vignette → excluded before treatment (Wave 1 post-hoc; Wave 2 pre-quota)
  • But passing ≠ understanding the treatment content
  • How to assess whether these respondents were actually bad respondents?
  • This motivates our embedding-based manipulation check
  • Standard attention checks can’t distinguish someone who read carefully from someone who skimmed and guessed correctly

Embeddings as a Factual Manipulation check

  • Cosine Similarity becomes a measure of engagement with the treatment (recall of treatment language)
  • Quantifiable and more accurate than a yes/no question
  • Allows for pre-treatment exclusion before quotas in wave 2.
  • We use OpenAI’s text-embedding-3-large (3072-dimensional vectors)
  • Each respondent’s writing task is embedded
  • We compute cosine similarity with the treatment text
  • Higher similarity = writing reflects the treatment’s semantic content

Text-embedding-3-large

Transformer Embedding model, released in 2023

  • We use OpenAI’s text-embedding-3-large (3072-dimensional vectors)
  • Each respondent’s writing task is embedded
  • We compute cosine similarity with the treatment text
  • Higher similarity = writing reflects the treatment’s semantic content

Cosine Similarity

\[ \text{sim}(\mathbf{r}_i, \mathbf{t}) = \frac{\mathbf{r}_i \cdot \mathbf{t}}{\|\mathbf{r}_i\| \, \|\mathbf{t}\|} \in [-1, 1] \]

Where \(\mathbf{r}_i\) is the embedding of respondent \(i\)’s response and \(\mathbf{t}\) the treatment embedding, both in \(\mathbb{R}^{3072}\) (text-embedding-3-large). Values approaching 1 indicate near-identical semantic content.

Validation: unattentive respondents evoke significantly less the treatment than attentive respondents

  • Cosine similarity measures how semantically close two texts are
  • Does NOT require verbatim repetition — captures paraphrasing and synonyms
  • Ranges from -1 (completely different) to 1 (identical meaning)
  • Crucial: we can check if excluded respondents were also semantically less treated

Results

Means for Outparty Thermometer

  • Effect is modest but significant after controlling for partisanship and excluding inattentive respondents
  • Comparable in magnitude to Levendusky (2018): β = 0.056
  • This is the first successful replication of that effect

OLS Coefficients

  • Wave 2 collected 6 months later, different panel provider
  • Effect disappears — not just smaller, but essentially zero
  • First instinct: something went wrong with the treatment delivery

Marginal Effects

  • Marginal effects analysis by party identification
  • Effect is statistically significant for Democrats
  • Republicans show a positive (non-significant) trend — not a backlash
  • Interpretation: civic/pluralist framing resonates more with Democrats in current polarized context

What explains the null?

Ruling out inattentiveness

  • If respondents in Wave 2 weren’t engaging with the treatment, their writing should be semantically distant from it
  • The embeddings show comparable similarity scores across waves
  • The null is real — not an artifact of poor data quality

Ruling out design-based explanations

  • Failed manipulation: same treatment produced significant effect in Wave 1
  • Poor measurement of Y: same thermometer instrument across waves
  • Treatment worked well the first time, excluding design-based problems. Similar designs have also worked recently, so they work.

Plausible explanations

  • Lack of power: Real effects are very small - to reliably detect them we would need exponentially more power.
  • Pre-treated respondents: Something changed from July 2025 to February 2026 that increased baseline levels of patriotism (visible in means)
  • Countervailing treatment effects : The treatment itself may be polarizing among Democrats, dilluting effects in wave 2.
  • Effects could be really small - thermometers are a single measure, so we could have noisy measures. Still, other DVs that tried to measure those concepts did not move either.

Implications

CIIM interventions may not be a reliable tool, even when isolated

  • Even doing considerable efforts to make a better treatment we obtain modest and unstable effects.

  • The increment and crystallization of Affective Polarization may make this kind of intervention more difficult to carry out in the future

  • Primes could integrate different parts to obtain a stronger effect (different national frames, attitude polarization correction, tired majority references, etc.)

  • Even a clean Patriotism prime produces modest, unstable effects
  • Mixed primes (Levendusky, Voelkel) compound this instability
  • Future designs should isolate frames before scaling up

Embeddings give us a new tool for experimental studies

  • By obtaining a continuous Factual Manipulation Check, we can assess with better precision how well the manipulation performed within groups of people and across studies.

  • While they should not be used themselves to exclude participants, they allow for a better assesment of inattentive respondents.

Conclusions

  1. A clean Patriotism prime produces a modest effect in Wave 1, replicating Levendusky
  2. The effect does not replicate in Wave 2, despite equal treatment engagement
  3. This null does not seem to be caused by inattentiveness or design
  4. Embedding-based manipulation checks are a promising tool for diagnosing null results
  • The substantive takeaway: Patriotism priming is not a reliable antidote for affective polarization
  • The methodological takeaway: we have a better way to rule out inattentiveness as an explanation
  • Next steps: more waves, explore Nationalism condition, pre-register the embedding check protocol
