Introduction

As Independence Day approaches and our nation looks ahead to its 250th anniversary, the nonprofit organization National Heritage has shared the following statements on social media.

Please read each text carefully and share your thoughtful impressions. Your feedback is greatly appreciated.

1 of 4 — A Country Built by Courage

From the pioneers who braved the wilderness to the soldiers who landed on the beaches of Normandy, America was carved out by strength, faith, and sacrifice.

We became the greatest nation on Earth not by chance, but because our people believed in hard work, family, and freedom.

Pioneers, soldiers, farm workers: they are all symbols of our strength, courage, and resilience.

Yet today, we see those values under attack.

2 of 4 — Standing Tall Against the World’s Threats

Across the globe, hostile regimes and shadowy terror networks test our resolve, seeking to weaken the freedoms we cherish. Some exploit our generosity, taking American jobs and prosperity while offering little in return.

When danger rises, Americans have always rallied to defend our own—on distant battlefields, at sea, and here at home. We must remain vigilant so that those who envy our liberty do not take it from us.

We stand watch so the homeland’s light never dims.

3 of 4 — A Legacy Worthy of Protection

Our nation’s bedrock rests on European, Judeo-Christian principles of law, faith, and individual dignity—ideals that built our monuments, guided our founders, and still draw millions who admire our way of life.

From snow-covered farm fields to Main Street parades, from country chapels to Arlington’s silent rows, America’s story is a testament: freedom defended is freedom preserved. The world may watch in awe, but our first duty is to look after Americans, ensuring that future generations inherit a country as strong and proud as the one we were given.

Our God-given, Western heritage of faith, law, and valor makes America unmatched in the world.

4 of 4 — This Is Your Country. Don’t Let Them Take It.

Today, as ever, our strength depends on ordinary citizens who refuse to let America’s torch dim. Work hard, honor those who served, uphold faith and family, and support policies that put Americans first.

When we protect our heritage, the promise of this nation endures.

We protect what is important. We teach what is important. Only like that we keep being free.

We need you to defend what is important. Our country needs you to keep being the greatest nation on earth.

Writing Task

Please summarize the key points in National Heritage’s July 4th Independence Day message. According to the organization, what are some of the best things about the United States?

Debrief

Thank you for taking part in our study.

We investigated how different portrayals of American national identity—some emphasizing traditional heritage and sacrifice, others highlighting civic unity and diversity—shape people's feelings toward fellow citizens and their willingness to engage in civic life. The nonprofit organization mentioned in the materials is fictional, and the texts and images you saw were created solely for research purposes; they were never posted on social media. To ensure natural reactions, we did not disclose these details in advance, and participants were randomly assigned to view one of several versions before completing questions and writing tasks.

All responses are stored without personal identifiers, analyzed only in aggregate form, and kept securely for at least five years in accordance with university and federal guidelines. If you have questions about the study, your rights as a participant, or the findings, please contact the principal investigator, Dr. Rongbo Jin, at jinrongbo@cuhk.edu.cn or the university's ethics board at fssc02@cuhk.edu.hk. We sincerely appreciate your time and insights, which will help us understand how different patriotic messages can foster healthier civic dialogue.

Introduction

As Independence Day approaches and our nation looks ahead to its 250th anniversary, the nonprofit organization We the People has shared the following statements on social media.

Please read each text carefully and share your thoughtful impressions. Your feedback is greatly appreciated.

1 of 4 — A Nation Built on an Ideal

America is so much more than a place. It’s a promise.

A promise that anyone, regardless of their background, can pursue life, liberty, and happiness under the rule of law.

That promise is what drew millions to our shores, and what continues to inspire us today.

One promise binds us—liberty under the law for every American.

2 of 4 — Unity Through Shared Values

Americans don’t need to look the same, pray the same, or vote the same to belong here.

What binds us all is belief in liberty, the Constitution, democracy, and equal justice under the law. And we live it out when we vote, speak up, and show up for each other.

Many faces, many beliefs, one democracy in action.

These values are not perfect. But they are worth improving and worth defending. Together.

3 of 4 — A Legacy of Freedom and Innovation

These values help us achieve great things—and redefine what’s possible. America gave the world the airplane, the moon landing, the internet, and the light bulb. We are the world’s oldest working democracy, and we have the freest press.

Freedom fuels our breakthroughs—from moon rockets to students coding the future.

You can see that spirit every day: students building robots, nurses thinking on their feet, mayors running food drives, neighbors starting businesses in their garages.

4 of 4 — We the People Still Matter

In moments of crisis—from civil wars to pandemics—ordinary Americans have always risen to the occasion. We have put our shared values over our differences.

In every crisis, We the People stand shoulder-to-shoulder—feeding one another, sustaining our democracy through shared care.

And now, we must do it again—by listening more, judging less, and putting our shared identity as Americans first.

Our democracy depends on us. We The People.
